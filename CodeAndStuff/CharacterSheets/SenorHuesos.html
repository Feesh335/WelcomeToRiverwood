Señor Huesos

In life, Señor Huesos was a bard fascinated with the dead and believed that they didn't deserve the somber dirges that people had grown so accustomed to playing to mourn them, and instead learned lively songs so catchy that even the dead danced along. As time went on and Huesos's renown increased, eventually death itself heard tales of this legendary bard who played for the dead. Fascinated by him, death offered a challenge: make me dance to your tune and I'll grant you a wish. Huesos spent the rest of his lifetime in pursuit of a song to regale death. Years passed to no avail, and Huesos found himself on his deathbed, he offered his hand for one last dance and was overjoyed when death took it. Expecting the usual wish of riches, immortality, or favors for some loved ones, death was surprised when Huesos wished to retain sentience as a skeleton but happily obliged. Even now, the dead wander towards Huesos and his songs, and he's been hired by the collector to attract some of the more interesting dead to the Collection.

Vicious Mockery (Costs 1 action): Target must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or take 1d4 psychic damage and have disadvantage on the next attack roll it makes before the end of the turn.
