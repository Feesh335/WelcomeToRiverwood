Alanna Crenworth, "The Priestess"

Daughter of the mayor of Riverwood and high priestess of the Mundus Faithful. From a young age she had a rebellious streak and a strong interest in the Mundus Faithful. Her father allowed her to go off to Narlan's Priory to be trained as a priestess of the Mundus Faithful. Father Narlan quickly took interest in her once she demonstrated her natural talent in the ways of the faith and took her in as an apprentice where she quickly rose through the ranks. The times were quiet and peaceful and Alanna became a close friend with Sister Abernathy, the only priestess she truly trusted. Once the Ordo Novis arrived in Southern Mollinstead, she quickly grew to hate them and frequently found herself in debates with her father, who felt little choice but to work with the Ordo Novis due to their power and influence. Rather than working with them diplomatically, Alanna set out to spy on the Ordo Novis to prove that they did not have the region's interests in mind and set out to gather dirt on Meridan Seelie, who made little effort to hide his true intentions. Her big break came when Meridan brought the sigil stone back to his tower and began bragging to his assistants about what he would do once he mastered the power contained within it, but before she could head back to Riverwood, she was caught in the blastwave of the sigil's activation. Although her body was utterly destroyed, her soul found its way into a binding rune that Meridan had kept on a shelf in his tower and found herself waking up as a Bound. Although disgusted with her new form which went against everything she believed in, she found her true purpose in the wake of the tragedy: to rally the Mundus Faithful and bring a permanent end to the defilers. She crafted a new robe and donned a mask to hide her new form and set out to the Arcane Realm to stop Meridan, occasionally stopping by Narlan's priory to talk with Sister Abernathy.

Passive

Rage: In battle, you fight with primal ferocity. On your turn, you can enter a rage as a bonus action. While raging, you gain the following benefits if you aren't wearing heavy armor: • You have advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws. • When you make a melee weapon attack using Strength, you gain a bonus to the damage roll that increases as you gain levels as a barbarian, as shown in the Rage Damage column of the Barbarian table. • You have resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage. If you are able to cast spells, you can't cast them or concentrate on them while raging. Your rage lasts for 1 minute. It ends early if you are knocked unconscious or if your turn ends and you haven't attacked a hostile creature since your last turn or taken damage since then. You can also end your rage on your turn as a bonus action. Once you have raged the number of times shown for your barbarian level in the Rages column of the Barbarian table, you must finish a long rest before you can rage again.
